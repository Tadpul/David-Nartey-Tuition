Teaching Philosophy

Understanding first. Grades follow.

My teaching is built around genuine understanding rather than memorisation — because a student who understands why something works can handle a question they've never seen before.

Understanding over memorising

We build ideas from first principles, so knowledge sticks because it makes sense — not because it was repeated enough times.

Deriving, not just using, formulas

Seeing where a formula comes from makes it intuitive — and far easier to recall and apply under exam pressure.

Logical reasoning and problem-solving

We practise breaking unfamiliar problems into smaller, solvable steps — a skill that transfers well beyond one exam.

Confidence with the unfamiliar

By the time exam season arrives, students shouldn't just know the syllabus — they should feel ready for the questions they haven't seen before.

"Strong grades are a natural consequence of deep understanding. When students know why something works, they are far better prepared for challenging exam questions."
